### Why "Unlimited Users" Matters
Valos charges per-firm, not per-valuer. For a firm like Eddisons (100 valuers):
- **Valos model:** One annual licence covers all 100 valuers — flat cost regardless of team size
- **PropVal model at £35/seat:** Would be £3,500/month (£42k/year) for hosting alone, before cases

This makes Valos attractive for large teams. But PropVal delivers fundamentally more value (intelligence + reports, not just reports), so the per-seat charge is justified — if the product proves it.

## Commercial Model Analysis

### What Valos Gets Right
1. **Annual contracts** — predictable revenue, lower churn risk than monthly
2. **Unlimited users** — removes friction for firm-wide adoption
3. **Enterprise sales motion** — CRO from CoStar hired early 2024 to drive deals
4. **Price opacity** — enables price discrimination by client size and willingness to pay
5. **No public pricing** — prevents competitors from undercutting on price alone

### What This Creates as Vulnerability
1. **Long sales cycles** — enterprise deals require demos, procurement, legal
2. **High CAC** — need salespeople (CRO, 2+ sales staff) to close each deal
3. **Opaque = friction** — small firms may not bother enquiring
4. **No self-serve** — can't onboard a solo surveyor who finds them online
5. **Transparent competitors win on trust** — PropVal's published pricing (£35/seat + £35/case) lets firms budget instantly without a sales call

## Key Insight

Valos's pricing model is optimised for the clients they already have — mid-to-large surveying firms with procurement departments and annual budgets. It is not optimised for the long tail of small practices and individual valuers, which is where PropVal's transparent per-seat + per-case model has a structural advantage. The question is which market is bigger: 50 enterprise firms paying £50k, or 1,000 valuers paying £35/month + £35/case.
